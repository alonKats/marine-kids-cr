# Journey School — Meeting Prep

**Read the first 3 sections 20 minutes before the meeting. The rest is for reference — use it if a question lands there.**

---

## 0. Pre-meeting checklist (do these BEFORE you walk in)

Two things can quietly kill this deal if you don't resolve them first:

1. **Which campus is this actually?** Journey School is a franchise with three public campuses: Tamarindo, Jacó, Costa Ballena. **"Playa Negra" is not on their public website.** Before the meeting, confirm:
   - Which physical location are they running?
   - Does that campus currently hold **IB accreditation**, or is it still pursuing it (like Jacó / Costa Ballena)?
   - If it's NOT IB-accredited yet → soften the CAS pitch. Lead with MEP biodiversity + PYP. Save CAS as "something for when you're fully accredited."
2. **Liability coverage.** Any fieldwork with children on a beach or near water raises insurance questions. A pedagogical manager will think about this even if they don't ask. If they DO ask — you need a one-sentence answer. If you don't yet carry personal liability coverage for school fieldwork, resolve this (talk to Alon / INS) before the meeting. This is the one topic that can silently kill a deal that otherwise felt ready.

If either of these isn't resolved, the rest of the prep still stands — just ask yourself whether to go in this week or buy a few days.

## 1. Goal (one sentence)

**Listen.** You have a pilot vision ready in your pocket — but let them describe their world before you put it on the table. Information first, offer second.

## 2. Context — why you initiated

- **You contacted Journey**, not the other way around. That sets the posture: curious, prepared, proposing something — not responding to a brief.
- **Why them:** their own mission statement — *"nurture the whole child through meaningful experiences, authentic relationships, and a deep connection to the natural world"* — is already 80% of your brief. They've built the frame; the ocean content is the empty room. **Mirror their words back.** "Connection to the natural world" and "meaningful experiences" are their phrases, not yours — use them.
- **Attendees** — the head + pedagogical managers (IB-coordinator equivalents, if this campus has one). One is strategic, the others are practical. Answer each accordingly.
- **"IB credits"** = CAS hours (Creativity, Activity, Service). Marine fieldwork + stewardship projects fit 5 of the 7 CAS learning outcomes directly. Strong natural entry — but let them raise it first, and only if this campus is actually IB-accredited.
- **Their staff:** Tamarindo has two IB science teachers publicly listed — **Juan Gabriel Sojo Quesada** and a colleague named Nancy. If your campus connects to Tamarindo, these are the people you'd likely co-teach with, not the managers you're meeting.

## 3. Your vision (in your pocket, not on the table)

A **year-long, school-embedded Ocean Literacy program**, piloted at Journey, aligned to IB PYP / MEP, anchored in the 7 Ocean Literacy Principles (NOAA / NMEA):

- **Early grades (K–2):** habitat models, touch-based exploration, "who lives in the ocean" — hands-on, concrete.
- **Middle grades (3–5):** ecosystems, pollution, human impact, observation-based — concrete + conceptual.
- **Upper grades (6+ / IB):** a *research question* anchor, CAS-eligible fieldwork, stewardship projects — conceptual + independent.

One-line frame if asked: *"Diseñamos una currícula anual de Ocean literacy para toda la escuela, piloteada con ustedes, adaptada a cada nivel, alineada con IB y MEP."*

**Concrete image to hold:** The olive ridley turtles are nesting at Ostional right now — 45 minutes away, during the school year. A Grade 5 unit built around a nest count is real science, not a simulation. When the conversation lulls, that image is your anchor.

## 4. Questions to ask (pick 4–5, in this order)

1. **"Cuéntenme cómo manejan actualmente la educación sobre el mar y el medio ambiente — ¿qué están haciendo, y qué sienten que falta?"**
   *(How do you currently handle marine / environmental education — what are you doing, and what feels missing?)*

2. **"¿Qué programas externos están usando hoy con los niños? ¿Qué funcionó? ¿Qué no?"**
   *(What external programs do you use today? What worked? What didn't?)* — surfaces competition + past frustrations.

3. **"Si armamos una currícula anual de Ocean literacy, ¿cómo encajaría con su calendario IB / MEP — bloques semanales, días especiales, campamentos, o una mezcla?"**
   *(If we build an annual Ocean literacy curriculum, how would it fit your IB / MEP calendar?)* — lets them tell you the shape.

4. **"¿Cómo manejan las horas CAS para IB? ¿Dónde tienen huecos?"** *(Only if this campus is IB-accredited and they open the thread first.)*

5. **"¿Qué niveles tendrían más prioridad — los pequeños, los IB, o un programa transversal que toque todos?"**
   *(Which levels would be highest priority — young ones, IB, or a transversal program?)* — tells you where the energy is.

## 5. Possibilities to name-drop (only if the conversation opens room)

Journey is prestige-leaning — international program names land. Drop them as **references we can draw from**, not commitments.

### The 4 anchors

- **FEE Eco-Schools (Green Flag).** Free, 100+ countries, 54,000+ schools, Spanish materials. The surprise: **the Green Flag is a credential that shows up in MSA / Cognia accreditation portfolios.** Not a sticker — a credential. If they care about accreditation, this is the drop-in:
  > *"Hay un camino para ganar la Green Flag de FEE — es una credencial internacional de educación ambiental que aparece en los portafolios de MSA y Cognia. Podría ser un entregable concreto del primer año."*
- **NOAA Marine Debris.** Free, downloadable K–12 curriculum, no registration. The real hook: CR's coast is part of actual NOAA-monitored datasets — upper-grade students doing beach surveys would be **contributing to a US federal science program**, not doing a classroom exercise.
- **Monterey Bay Aquarium.** Free core curriculum, peer-reviewed by marine scientists, the same institution California public schools use. Drop the name for credibility — *"podemos inspirarnos del marco de Monterey Bay"* — without committing to adoption.
- **Nautilus Live (Ocean Exploration Trust, Robert Ballard's ship).** Free, Spanish materials, **live ship-to-shore Q&A 7 days/week May–September.** Students ask questions in real time to scientists on the ocean floor. The drop-in:
  > *"Hay un programa que se llama Nautilus Live — el barco de Robert Ballard, exploración en vivo del fondo marino, con Q&A en vivo para escuelas y materiales en español. Podríamos conectar a sus alumnos de IB con científicos en tiempo real en el segundo semestre."*

### Ready Spanish framing (safe version — reuse verbatim)

> *"Existen programas internacionales como FEE, NOAA Marine Debris, Monterey Bay y Nautilus — podemos inspirarnos de ellos y adaptar el contenido para Journey. Nada prometido, solo posibilidades."*

**Inspired by, tailored for you.** Do not promise integration or partnership.

### The turtle calendar (your strongest concrete asset)

Keep this in mind — it's the single best image you can drop:

- **Olive ridley nesting:** August–November (peak), Ostional National Wildlife Refuge. Arribadas of hundreds of thousands of turtles. 45 min from Playa Negra / Tamarindo.
- **Leatherback (Critically Endangered):** October–March, Las Baulas National Marine Park. 15 min from Tamarindo.

Both happen **during the school year**. No PowerPoint beats *"the turtles are nesting 20 minutes from here, and your Grade 7 class can document it."*

## 6. The 7 Ocean Literacy Principles (NOAA / NMEA)

If they want to understand what "Ocean Literacy" actually is, the answer is:

1. The Earth has one big ocean with many features.
2. The ocean and life in the ocean shape the features of Earth.
3. The ocean is a major influence on weather and climate.
4. The ocean made the Earth habitable.
5. The ocean supports a great diversity of life and ecosystems.
6. The ocean and humans are inextricably interconnected.
7. The ocean is largely unexplored.

45 supporting concepts, grade-banded scope & sequence (K–2 / 3–5 / 6–8 / 9–12). This is the framework, not my invention. They map directly onto **IB PYP "Sharing the Planet"** and **MEP Ciencias Naturales biodiversity standards**.

The MEP angle worth saying out loud: *"MEP mandates que se enseñe biodiversidad marina, pero la mayoría de las escuelas lo cumplen con contenido terrestre porque no tienen un biólogo marino. Yo lleno ese vacío."*

## 7. If they ask (pocket answers — quick reference)

Keep these short. Don't volunteer them — use them if asked.

- **"How is this different from field trips?"** → Field trips are events. This is a curriculum — 36 sessions that build on each other. A trip teaches wonder; a curriculum teaches science.
- **"How do you measure learning?"** → International Ocean Literacy Survey (IOLS) pre/post for older students, NOAA Ocean Literacy Scope & Sequence rubrics for younger grades. Validated, globally used. You'll have a measured learning gain for parents and accreditors.
- **"Who pays? Per student or per school?"** → *"Les mando una propuesta esta semana. Mi modelo típico es un fee anual por escuela, no por alumno — predecible para presupuestar."* Don't quote a number in the room.
- **"What if you can't make it one week?"** → Every session has a teacher guide so your classroom teacher can run a follow-up. Plus one buffer week per semester built in.
- **"Accreditation?"** → MSA and Cognia look for experiential learning, community engagement, sustainability. Program satisfies all three. FEE Green Flag is a credential you can build toward.
- **"Our teachers aren't marine biologists."** → That's the design. I run the science; teachers handle continuity. By Year 2, I've trained them to facilitate ~40% independently.
- **"We already use MarViva / PRETOMA / turtle tours."** → Excellent programs — I know them. They're episodic: a visit, a day. I offer narrative thread across a year. Different educational experience.
- **"Can we do a trial?"** → Yes, and I'd encourage it. A semester pilot (18 sessions). Review assessment data together, decide about the full year.
- **"Is it CAS-eligible?"** *(only if IB-accredited)* → Yes — satisfies LO2 (new skills), LO3 (student-initiated), LO5 (collaborative), LO6 (global significance), LO7 (ethics). I provide activity descriptions + reflection prompts that meet IB requirements.
- **"How do we know you'll be here Year 2?"** → I live here. Family is rooted here. Program is designed to be renewable, and I build teacher capacity so the school keeps the framework regardless.

## 8. Red team — what could kill this (and how to preempt)

These are their real objections. Don't wait to be asked — seed the preempt into the conversation.

| Their real concern | Preempt |
|---|---|
| **"We're already full, no room in the timetable."** | Frame as **integration**, not addition. "I fit into your existing science or outdoor-ed block — I'm not asking for a new slot, I'm offering to fill one that runs thin on marine content." |
| **"We've been burned by outside programs before."** | Offer the semester pilot explicitly. Bring written structure (curriculum outline, session-by-session). Don't just claim reliability — prove it with structure. |
| **"Who handles liability?"** | *"Llevo seguro de responsabilidad personal y preparo un waiver para padres. Se los mando esta semana."* (Assumes you have coverage — resolve before the meeting if not.) |
| **"This cost wasn't budgeted."** | *"Entiendo el ciclo presupuestario. Si no es para agosto, podemos planear un piloto de segundo semestre en enero."* Don't force a timeline. |
| **"Parents will push back on a new fee."** | Offer a parent-communication draft they can use. Two paragraphs on what Ocean Literacy is and why it matters for CR families. Makes their job easier. |
| **"Maybe Tamarindo campus should decide first."** | Know the accreditation status before you walk in. If it's a newer campus, lead with MEP + PYP, not CAS — don't ask a Jacó head to make an IB-level call. |

## 9. What to listen for

The one sentence where they describe a problem they can't solve themselves. Write it down word-for-word. That sentence becomes the opening line of your written proposal.

Likely candidates to listen for:
- A specific grade level that's under-served
- An accreditation deliverable they're chasing
- A parent-communication or community-pride need
- A teacher capacity gap ("our teachers don't know how to teach marine science")

---

## 10. What NOT to commit to yet

**No prices. No dates. No specific curriculum lessons.** If they push:

> *"Me encantaría pensarlo con cuidado y mandarles una propuesta esta semana — quiero que sea algo hecho a medida para Journey, no una plantilla."*

Buy the week.

**Exception: liability.** If they ask about fieldwork safety / insurance, give a one-sentence answer (*"sí, llevo seguro y preparo waivers — va en la propuesta"*) — don't deflect this one. It's a dealbreaker, not a detail.
