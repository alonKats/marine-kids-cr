# Journey School — Meeting Prep

**Read this 30 minutes before the meeting.**

---

## Goal of this meeting (one sentence)

**Listen.** Find out what Journey actually needs from a marine educator — then we build the offer after. Not today.

---

## What we know going in

- **Journey School** — Playa Negra campus, close to you (~15 min). Founded 2016 Tamarindo, added Jacó campus later.
- **Accreditations you'll hear about** — IB (they got authorized Dec 2023), MEP (CR ministry), MSA (US accreditor — this is the "middle country organisation"), Cognia. They're proud of this stack.
- **"IB credits"** — this is **CAS hours** (Creativity, Activity, Service). IB students must log them. Marine science outdoor programs = strong CAS fit. This is your natural entry point, but **don't bring it up first — let them**.
- **Their style** — project-based, Reggio Emilia-inspired, dual-language, small ratios (10:1). Jacó campus already emphasizes CR biodiversity. They lean experiential.

---

## Questions to ask (pick 4–5, in this order)

1. **"Cuéntenme qué los trajo a esta reunión — ¿qué les hizo pensar en traer ciencias marinas a la escuela?"**
   *(Tell me what brought you to this meeting — what made you think about bringing marine science to the school?)* — Opens them up. Let them talk.

2. **"¿Qué programas externos están usando ahora con los niños? ¿Qué funcionó? ¿Qué no?"**
   *(What external programs are you using now? What worked? What didn't?)* — Tells you who your competition is and what they're tired of.

3. **"¿Cómo manejan las horas de CAS para los estudiantes de IB? ¿Dónde tienen huecos?"**
   *(How do you handle CAS hours for IB students? Where are the gaps?)* — **Only ask once they've opened the IB thread themselves.** This surfaces the real unmet need.

4. **"¿Qué edades tendrían más interés — primaria, secundaria, o ambas?"**
   *(Which ages would be most interested — primary, secondary, or both?)* — Shapes the program, not the sale.

5. **"Si tuvieran que elegir entre un programa semanal, un día especial, o un campamento de verano, ¿qué encajaría mejor con su calendario?"**
   *(If you had to pick between a weekly program, a special day, or a summer camp — what would fit your calendar?)* — Lets them tell you the shape. Don't propose one.

---

## What to listen for

The one sentence where they describe a problem they can't solve themselves — that's your opening. Write it down word-for-word.

---

## What NOT to commit to yet

**No prices. No dates. No program details.** If they push — "me encantaría pensarlo con cuidado y mandarles una propuesta esta semana" *(I'd love to think it through carefully and send you a proposal this week)*. Buy the week.
