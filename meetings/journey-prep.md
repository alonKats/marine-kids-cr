# Journey School — Meeting Prep

**Read this 20 minutes before the meeting. *You* reached out to *them* — you're driving.**

---

## Goal (one sentence)

**Listen.** You have a pilot vision ready in your pocket — but let them describe their world before you put it on the table. Information first, offer second.

---

## Context — why you initiated

- **You contacted Journey**, not the other way around. That sets the posture: curious, prepared, proposing something — not responding to a brief.
- **Why them:** Playa Negra campus is ~15 min from you. IB + MEP + MSA + Cognia stack — they're proud of the accreditation layer. Reggio-inspired, project-based, dual-language, CR-biodiversity emphasis (Jacó). Their values already overlap with Ocean literacy.
- **Attendees** — the head + pedagogical managers (IB-coordinator equivalents). One is strategic, the others are practical. Answer each accordingly.
- **"IB credits"** = CAS hours (Creativity, Activity, Service). Marine science outdoor programs are strong CAS fits. Natural entry point — but let them raise it first.

---

## Your vision (in your pocket, not on the table)

A **year-long, school-wide Ocean literacy program**, **piloted at Journey**, tailored to their calendar and grade bands:

- **Early grades (K–2):** habitat models, touch-based exploration, "who lives in the ocean" — hands-on, concrete.
- **Middle grades (3–5):** ecosystems, pollution, human impact, observation-based — concrete + conceptual.
- **Upper grades (6+ / IB):** a *research question* anchor, CAS-eligible field work, stewardship projects — conceptual + independent.

One-line frame if asked: *"Diseñamos una currícula anual de Ocean literacy para toda la escuela, piloteada con ustedes, adaptada a cada nivel."*

---

## Questions to ask (pick 4–5, in this order)

1. **"Cuéntenme cómo manejan actualmente la educación sobre el mar y el medio ambiente — ¿qué están haciendo, y qué sienten que falta?"**
   *(How do you currently handle marine / environmental education — what are you doing, and what feels missing?)* — Opens their current state without assuming gaps.

2. **"¿Qué programas externos están usando hoy con los niños? ¿Qué funcionó? ¿Qué no?"**
   *(What external programs do you use today? What worked? What didn't?)* — Surfaces competition + past frustrations.

3. **"Si armamos una currícula anual de Ocean literacy, ¿cómo encajaría con su calendario IB / MEP — bloques semanales, días especiales, campamentos, o una mezcla?"**
   *(If we build an annual Ocean literacy curriculum, how would it fit your IB / MEP calendar?)* — Lets them tell you the shape. Don't propose one.

4. **"¿Cómo manejan las horas CAS para IB? ¿Dónde tienen huecos?"**
   *(How do you handle CAS hours for IB? Where are the gaps?)* — Only once they've opened the IB / accreditation thread.

5. **"¿Qué niveles tendrían más prioridad — los pequeños, los IB, o un programa transversal que toque todos?"**
   *(Which levels would be highest priority — the young ones, IB, or a transversal program covering all?)* — Tells you where the energy is.

---

## Possibilities to name-drop (only if the conversation opens room)

Journey is a prestige-leaning school — international program names will land. Drop them as **references we can draw from**, not commitments:

- **FEE** — Foundation for Environmental Education (Eco-Schools, Blue Flag). International, recognized in the IB world.
- **NOAA Marine Debris** — US-agency K–12 lesson plans, freely available.
- **Monterey Bay Aquarium** — gold-standard marine curriculum, global reach.
- **Nautilus Live** — Ocean Exploration Trust, live-feed deep-sea science.

Ready-to-use framing in Spanish:

> *"Existen programas internacionales como FEE, NOAA Marine Debris, Monterey Bay y Nautilus — podemos inspirarnos de ellos y adaptar el contenido para Journey. Nada prometido, solo posibilidades."*

**Inspired by, tailored for you.** Do not promise integration or partnership.

---

## What to listen for

The one sentence where they describe a problem they can't solve themselves. Write it down word-for-word. That sentence becomes the opening line of your proposal.

---

## What NOT to commit to yet

**No prices. No dates. No curriculum details.** If they push:

> *"Me encantaría pensarlo con cuidado y mandarles una propuesta esta semana — quiero que sea algo hecho a medida para Journey, no una plantilla."*

Buy the week.
